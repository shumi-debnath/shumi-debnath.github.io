Anatomy of a Typeface

Anatomy of a Typeface is a san serif typeface that can be used for headings or used as a decorative font. I also believe this typeface can be used when making a movie title sequence, whether it's a mystery movie or a thriller movie. When making this typeface, I wanted to start off with a black box and use different shapes to carve out from the box to create that letter. I also wanted it asymmertrical and off-centered the overlapped shapes, more to the right side. I utilized shapes like, semi-circle, rectangles, triangles and squares. When working with colors, since the typeface can work in a movie title sequence or posters, the colors were inspired by colors used by Saul Bass, in his movie sequences or posters. When hovered over, the colors not only invert on each letter, the color gets brighter. It also spins for two reasons, to mimic his Vertigo movie poster and to mimic a movie title sequence. I chose Anatomy of a Typeface to be the name because one of his most famous movie poster was "Anatomy of a Murder".
